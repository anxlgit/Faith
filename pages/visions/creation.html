Hi.
I'm Anxl.
Let's talk about creation.

First, let me get this out:
I'm a nihilist.

That doesn't mean I'm pessimistic,
nor do I hate the world.

It only means I don't find life
to have ant inherent pupose.

I find life to be little less
than a biological by-product.

I think the universe is cold,
immense,
and mostly dark.

I think that we're alone,
and have no real reason to exist.

These thoughts are terrifying. Our brains just can't take the cruelty.

And that's why we make execuses.
And that's why we make art.

Hold up. Let me explain.

Through the years, art was deemed worthless,
or even worse, a waste of time.

But I disagree.

I think art has been our best way
to fight the chaos of the universe.

When I speak of art, however,
I don't mean it as an exercise,
but as a process.

And, in the same way,
when I speak about creation,
I think of it as a theraphy.

Our lives are small and random,
and full of difficult experiences.

Our languages, moreover, are limited.

Many emotions can't be explained,
nor conveyed easily through conversation.

Take the buzzing heat of evenings
in the days of summer.

Or the soft murmur of the leaves,
falling in silence.

Or the stolen kiss
of someone whom we deeply love.

These experiences are central to our lives.
They shape our selves.

But there's a problem.

As social animals, we need to share them.

We need to feel we're understood.
- to feel we're not alone.

But how to do this?

How to share the un-defined,
or un-definable?

The best solution, it turns out,
is to replicate.

To create our own experiences.

Hence creation.

This is my point: that,
when we make things,
what we're hoping for
is understanding.

But there's a problem here as well.

No work is unequivocal.
All experience is interpreted.

And interpretation always falls
under personal perspectives.

Different people come
with different backgrounds.

Or worldviews. Or ideals.

Some will agree with us.
Some will not.

Some will even hate us.

But worst of all,
most won't even care.

Of all the people in earth,
only a little fraction
will even be in the exact position
to take a peek at all we do.

This is why, at first,
nobody seems to pay attention.

Not because nobody cares,
but because we haven't reached
the ones that truely would.

It takes time to get there.

The process is messy.
Complicated.
At times, it will seem possible.
Chaotic. Even unjust.

But do you remember?

Our initial fight was just the same:
to simplify and understand,
a different form of chaos.

To fight against the universe. But still a fight, nevertheless.

In some weird way,
the cycle is eternal.

The fight is never-ending.

And that's just bonkers.

But also beautiful.

So my advice would be to make.

To stay fighting.
To keep creating.

And someday,
someday will get you.

I promise.

Now the question is:

What will you make?

Anxl done.
